Background of the Study
 Calvary Christian Academy was established by Christian couple, Pastor Carl Gormley and Mrs. Michelle Gormley, located at Blk 1 Lot 9 & 10, Sarmiento Townville Subdivison, Barangay Poblacion I, San Jose Del Monte City, Bulacan 3023. The school operates from Monday – Friday, between 6:00 AM and 5:00 PM, providing quality education and. The establishment offers Kinder up to Grade 10 basic education.
Educational institutions carry the fundamental responsibility of managing student data, academic progress, and financial records to ensure a high standard of learning and administrative efficiency. Accurate data management is the backbone of school operations, as it directly influences enrollment fluidity, parental communication, and the school’s ability to support student success. To maintain a competitive and organized environment, modern schools must ensure that information, ranging from personal birth records to sensitive academic grades and tuition history is stored securely and remains easily accessible to authorized personnel. Effective student monitoring not only aids in daily administration but also serves as a vital tool for educators and registrars to identify students' needs and maintain the institution's financial stability.
Despite the importance of organized data management, the current process remains heavily reliant on manual, repetitive, and fragmented methods. Student information is currently written across various forms six to ten times during enrollment, leading to significant encoding bottlenecks and human errors. Financial tracking is conducted through basic Excel spreadsheets and handwritten receipts, while general school data is scattered across physical filing cabinets, disconnected spreadsheets, and Google Drive folders. This lack of centralization forces administrative staff to spend approximately 10 to 15 hours per week simply managing paper records. Furthermore, generating comprehensive institutional reports is a labor-intensive task that takes between 10 to 12 hours, hindering the school’s ability to make timely, data-driven decisions regarding student performance and tuition collection.
To resolve these operational inefficiencies, this study proposes the development of a Web-Based AI-Assisted Student Information and Academic Monitoring System. The proposed system integrates Artificial Intelligence through four core components: Optical Character Recognition (OCR) for automated document data extraction during enrollment, Machine Learning for tuition payment risk prediction, an Early Warning System for detecting declining academic performance trends, and an Intelligent Report Generator for instant analytics. By digitizing student records and utilizing AI to analyze patterns in grades and payments, the system aims to reduce report generation time from hours to seconds, eliminate repetitive data entry, and provide proactive interventions for at-risk students. Ultimately, this system seeks to modernize school administration, ensuring a more efficient, accurate, and supportive environment for both staff and students.
