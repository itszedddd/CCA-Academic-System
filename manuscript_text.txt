 ABSTRACT  
 As Calvary Christian Academy experiences growth in its student 
population and administrative operations, the existing fragmented and 
manual system for managing student records, enrollment, tuition payments, 
and report generation has become inefficient, result ing in data redundancy, 
delays in processing, increased workload, and a higher risk of errors. This 
study developed an AI -Enhanced School Management System to 
streamline and improve the school’s data management processes.  
The system features a  centralized, web -based platform that securely 
stores and manages student information, enabling faster access and 
improved accuracy. It incorporates AI Auto -Fill Enrollment to reduce 
repetitive data entry, AI Error Detection to identify inconsistencies, an d an 
AI Report Generator to produce academic and financial reports instantly. A 
digital tuition tracking module was also implemented to enhance payment 
monitoring and record accuracy.  
The implementation of the system improved operational efficiency, 
reduce d administrative workload, minimized data redundancy, and 
enhanced the accessibility and reliability of records. It also enabled faster 
report generation and better monitoring of student and financial data, 
supporting more effective decision -making and ove rall school management.

 INTRODUCTION  
 
I. Background of the Study  
Calvary Christian Academy, established by Pastor Carl Gormley and 
Mrs. Michelle Gormley, is located at Blk 1 Lot 9 & 10, Sarmiento Townville 
Subdivision, Barangay Poblacion I, City of San Jose del Monte, Bulacan 
3023. The institution offers basic education  from Kindergarten to Grade 10 
and operates from Monday to Friday, 6:00 AM to 5:00 PM. The school has 
a workforce of 20 teachers and administrative personnel, including 1 
Principal, 2 Registrar Staff, 1 Cashier, 1 IT Staff, 1 Librar ian, 1 Guidance 
Counselor, and 1 School Nurse. It manages a wide range of records, 
including student academic performance, tuition payments, and personal 
information. Efficient and accurate record -keeping is essential to ensure 
smooth school operations, ti mely communication with parents, effective 
monitoring of student performance, and proper tracking of financial 
obligations.  
At present, the school maintains student records across multiple 
platforms, resulting in inefficiencies and redundancy. The enrollme nt 
process requires the completion of several forms, including the Student 
Information Form, ID Information Form, Data Privacy Consent Form,    
 
2 
 Medical History Form, and Waiver for Face -to-Face Classes. This leads to 
repeated encoding of the same student information approximately six (6) to 
ten (10) times across different documents. Based on gathered data, the 
enrollment process currently accommod ates around 1 –50 students per day 
during peak periods, with an average waiting time of 21 –30 minutes and a 
processing time of 11 –20 minutes per student. Delays are mainly caused 
by slow encoding, long queues, and incomplete requirements such as 
report card s. Additionally, 0 –10% of students submit incomplete 
requirements, and the longest part of the process is document checking. 
Tuition payments are tracked manually using receipts and recorded in 
spreadsheets such as Excel, which increases the risk of errors  and 
inconsistencies, with an estimated 0 –5 payment errors per month.  
Furthermore, record management inefficiencies affect both accuracy 
and workload. The system records approximately 6 –10 errors per month, 
with 2 –3 corrections needed per student on averag e. Common issues 
include misspelled names, duplicate records, and missing data, primarily 
caused by manual encoding. Student and parent information is also 
repeatedly encoded, leading to redundancy. Payment verification takes 
about 6 –10 minutes per transac tion, with challenges such as manual 
tracking, missing receipts, and human error. In addition, registrars spend 
approximately 10 to 15 hours per week managing and updating records,    
 
3 
 while report generation requires about 10 to 12 hours. Although teachers 
can access student grades and contact information via Google Drive, only 
registrars are authorized to edit records, and difficulties in locating student 
information occur occasionally . These limitations reduce efficiency in record 
retrieval, report generation, and overall administrative performance.  
To address these challenges, this study proposes the development 
of an AI -Enhanced School Management System. The proposed system will 
centralize student information, including academic records, tuition 
payments into a secure, cloud -based platform. It will incorporate key 
Artificial Intelligence (AI) features such as AI Auto -Fill Enrollment to 
minimize redundant data entry, AI Error Detection  to identify inconsistencies 
and missing information, and an AI Report Generator to produce instant 
academic and financial reports. These innovations aim to enhance 
efficiency, accuracy, and accessibility while reducing administrative 
workload, data redund ancy, and processing delays. Ultimately, the system 
seeks to allow school personnel to focus more on student development and 
the overall quality of education.

 II. Review of Related Literature, Studies, and Systems  
a. Review of Related Literature  
Foreign Literature  
The integration  of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and modern digital 
frameworks  into educational management has become a focal point for 
institutional transformation, particularly in addressing the inefficiencies of 
traditional, fragmented systems . Recent advancements demonstrate that 
AI-driven Student Management Information Systems (AISMIS)  can 
drastically optimize campus administration by shifting from decentralized, 
paper -based workflows to centralized, high -speed online platforms. These  
systems utilize big data analysis algorithms, such as Random Forest , to 
process and categorize complex datasets with significant precision and 
speed (Wang, 2024).  
Beyond basic data organization, the implementation of Hybrid AI -
based Prediction Models (Hybrid -AIPM)  allows for proactive academic 
performance monitoring by identifying at -risk students through Machine 
Learning Algorithms  (MLA)  (Wang, 2025). This transition is essential as 
traditional electronic systems often reach a performance plateau , lacking  
the computational robustness  required to handle the increasing volume and 
quantization  of modern institutional data (Wang, 2024) .    
 
5 
 In addition to AI, the adoption of blockchain technology  and 
advanced evaluative models offer critical solutions for data integrity and 
institutional organization. Blockchain provides a decentralized, "tamper -
proof" ledger system that secures student credentials across multiple 
nodes, addressing the inherent vu lnerabilities and lack of transparency 
found in traditional centralized databases (Kong, 2024).  
Complementary to these technical structures, the Input -Process -
Output (IPO) model serves as a vital fra mework for evaluating how 
educational inputs and administrative processes directly dictate student 
learning outcomes and overall institutional performance (Shaukat & Pell, 
2015). However, the success of such digital transformations is frequently 
hindered b y an "implementation failure" or an "authority -accountability gap," 
where institutions possess formal digital policies but lack the necessary IT 
infrastructure, staff skills, or localized administrative authority to 
operationalize them effectively (Yidana & Akuna, 2025; Perez & Malana, 
2023).  
User  acceptance remains a decisive factor in the long -term viability 
of these technological integrations . Meta -analytic  research suggests that 
"enjoyment" is the most critical driver of student technology adoption, 
although this is heavily influenced by cultural and contextual moderators 
(Santini et al., 2025). For educators, the transition to systems like Learning    
 
6 
 Management Systems (LMS)  is highly dependent on perceived ease of use  
and the availability of continuous technical support to overcome 
psychological and technical barriers (Aldosemani, 2023). Furthermore, 
research indicates that technology must demonstrate a strong "task -
technology fit," meaning the system's capabilities mu st align precisely with 
the specific academic requirements of the user to improve satisfaction and 
performance (Alyoussef, 2021).  
The integration of AI into management information systems  presents 
significant opportunities as well as challenges in higher education contexts. 
According to student perspectives at King Khalid University, AI integration 
enhances system capabilities such as data processing, forecasting, and 
decision support , leading to improved administrative efficiency. However, 
respondents also identified concerns related to data privacy, user 
readiness, and ethical considerations, indicating that successful 
implementation requires careful planning, training, and policies to mi tigate 
potential risks (Al‑Kindi Publishers, 2024).  
The construction of an AI Student Management Information System 
using big data analysis algorithms demonstrates how advanced 
computational techniques can improve data processing and decision -
making within educational environments.  This system leverages big data 
analytics to efficiently manage large volumes of student information,    
 
7 
 enhancing accuracy and system responsiveness compared to traditional 
methods. Findings from the study suggest that integrating AI and big data 
capabilities into Management Information System (MIS)  facilitates more 
effective handling of complex datasets, supports predictive insights, and 
strengthens institutional data management practices (IEEE, 2024).  
The design of student management assessment information 
systems using Artificial Neural Network (ANN)  algorithms illustrates the 
application of AI in enhancing the accuracy of academic evaluations and 
data analysis. ANN ￼based systems provide improved pattern recognition 
and predictive capabilities, which enable  more reliable assessment and 
interpretation of student performance data. This approach enhances 
decision support for administrators and educators by offering deeper 
insights into student progress and facilitating more targeted interventions 
(ACM, 2024).  
The implementation of AI combined with cloud computing  for student 
management information security strengthens the protection of sensitive 
educational data against unauthorized access and cyber threats . AI￼driven 
security mechanisms such as anomaly detection and real ￼time threat 
response enhance system robustness, while cloud computing enables 
scalable, centralized data storage. Research indicates that this combined 
approach improves system reliability, data integrity , and administrative    
 
8 
 control , making it an effective solution for securing student information 
systems (Springer, 2023).  
The implementation of effective IT governance frameworks is crucial 
for ensuring alignment between technology initiatives and institutional goals 
in higher education. A systematic  literature review of academic information 
systems highlights that robust governance structures improve system 
performance, risk management, accountability, and stakeholder 
satisfaction. By providing strategic oversight, governance f rameworks help 
institutions manage complexities associated with information systems, 
including security, compliance, and resource allocation (IEEE, 2021).  
Research examining the effect of management information systems 
on student academic performance in selected higher learning institutions in 
Lusaka District, Zambia, found that MIS plays a significant role in enhancing 
academic outcomes. The study revealed that access to timely information, 
improved communication, and effici ent data management positively 
influence both teaching and learning processes. These findings suggest 
that institutions leveraging effective MIS can better monitor student 
progress, identify at ‑risk students, and implement interventions that support 
improv ed academic performance (Chanda & Mubika, 2024).  
Research on the application of student information management 
systems in higher vocational colleges reveals their important role in

The study found that when properly  implemented, such systems enhance

The design and simulation of a computerized student information

systems has been show n to enhance the effectiveness of strategic

 AI‑driven MIS contributes to more informed decision ‑making processes,

 Local Literature  
The transition from manual record -keeping to digitized , intelligent 
systems is a  critical response to documented operational bottlenecks  in 
Philippine educational institutions, where educators face significant 
challenges in manual data entry  and information storage. These manual 
procedures often result in data inconsistencies, security risks, and slow 
retrieval speeds, necessitating the development of centralized platforms 
that can automate core functions like admissions and report generation  to 
improve administrative efficiency (Falolo et al., 2022).  
Foundational local efforts have su ccessfully implemented 
comprehensive e -school systems using structured information systems 
approaches to manage community college records, yet these early 
iterations often lacked the automated features necessary to eliminate heavy 
administrative oversight (Grepon et al., 2021) . Recent evidence from private 
preschool settings further confirms that such systems significantly minimize 
clerical errors and improve the speed of information retrieval compared to 
traditional manual methods, which is particularly re levant for small -scale 
institutions managing multi -step workflows (Gabayan & Sable -Gamboa, 
2025).  
The specific difficulties encountered by teachers in localized 
contexts —such as the high level of difficulty in entering and saving data —   
 
12 
 underscore the urgent need for systems that prioritize automated security 
and ease of use to prevent data loss or duplication (Riño & Daing, 2022). 
To address these gaps, the integration of mobile -based platforms and e -
payment systems has been shown to str eamline registrar operations, 
although research suggests these tools must be further enhanced with real -
time error -correction features to fully mitigate human -entry mistakes 
(Pagay -Cierva, 2024).  
The evolution of these systems is increasingly defined by th e 
application of the Input -Process -Output (IPO) model, which serves as a 
structural framework for transforming raw student data into actionable 
information, provided it is modernized with intelligent processing layers to 
ensure accuracy (University of the Philippines Open University, 2024).  
Beyond basic digitization, the incorporation of Artificial Intelligence 
(AI) and Large Language Models (LLMs)  offers powerful capabilities for 
real-time error detection and structural guidance, although they require 
huma n oversight to maintain pedagogical  and instructional integrity (Kim & 
Reeves, 2024).  
The sustainability of these AI -enhanced systems relies heavily on 
user acceptance; studies indicate that factors such as perceived visual 
attractiveness and trust are critical, as users often prioritize an intuitive    
 
13 
 interface  over back -end technical robustness  (Garcia & Palaoag, 2023; 
Saflor & Palaoag, 2025).  
Furthermore, the ultimate success of an institutional platform is 
determined by the "Task -Technology Fit," where the system's specific 
features —such as automated reporting or auto -fill enrollment —must be 
precisely mapped to the daily clerical tasks of the staff to improve 
performance and user satisfaction (Navarro et al., 2021; Hidayat & Palaoag, 
2024).  
This need for technical precision is further supported by rec ent 
advancements in hybrid AI -based prediction models that allow for highly 
accurate academic performance monitoring and early proactive intervention 
(Wang, 2025).  
The integration of violation management into student information 
systems enhances the efficiency of monitoring student behavior and 
maintaining accurate records. A study on the Student Information and 
Violation Management System (SIVMS)  showed that combining student 
data with violation tracking improves reporting and administrative decision -
makin g. The system reduces manual errors, streamlines documentation, 
and supports more organized management of student -related concerns in 
higher education institutions (Olipas, 2020).

 III. Statement of the Problem  
Calvary Christian Academy manages student enrollment, academic 
records, grading, and tuition payments across multiple platforms, where 
student information is repeatedly recorded in different forms, stored in 
separate locations, and report generation requir es a significant amount of 
time, leading to inefficiencies, errors, and delays in accessing important 
information; as the number of students and administrative tasks continues 
to increase, the school faces challenges in maintaining  accurate, organized, 
and easily accessible records, creating a need to improve efficiency, reduce 
redundant work, and speed up processes such as enrollment, tuition 
tracking, and report generation to support faster decision -making, better 
communication wi th parents, and smoother school operations.  
a. General Problem  
Calvary Christian Academy encounters difficulties in efficiently 
managing student records, grades, tuition payments, and report generation, 
resulting in increased administrative workload, repeated data entry, and 
delays in accessing and processing informat ion. 
    
 
39 
 b. Specific Problem s 
• Student information is recorded multiple times across different 
forms and files (6 –10 repetitions per student), causing data 
redundancy and increasing the risk of inconsistencies.  
• Accessing student records, grades, and tuition information takes 
10-15 minutes  per request due to multiple storage systems, 
resulting in reduced efficiency.  
• The preparation of academic and tuition -related reports takes 10 
to 12 hours, leading to delays in releasing information and making 
decisions.  
• Tuition payments are currently tracked using spreadsheets and 
physical receipts, which increases administrative workload and 
the likelihood of errors during verification, with an estimated five 
(5) errors occurring per month in payment records.  
• The enrollment process involves manual form filling and 
document handling, which slows down processing time and 
increases staff workload, particularly for older personnel who may 
find the process more time -consuming.  
• The absence of a centralized system results in inefficient data 
management and negatively impacts system accessibility, 
scalability, and overall performance.

 METHODOLOGY  
This section focuses on analyzing the existing administrative and 
record -keeping processes of Calvary Christian Academy and identifying the 
root causes of inefficiency in enrollment, academic record management, 
tuition tracking, and report generation. Base d on the results of interviews, 
observations  conducted among the principal, registrar, teachers, and 
administrative staff, several recurring issues were identified. These include 
redundant data entry across multiple forms (6 –10 repet itions per student), 
slow access to student records and tuition information due to fragmented 
storage, time -consuming report generation (10 –12 hours), manual tuition 
tracking using spreadsheets and receipts, and delays in enrollment 
processing. These findi ngs revealed the urgent need for a centralized, AI -
enhanced school management system capable of automating data entry, 
error detection, and report generation, thereby improving efficiency, 
accuracy, and accessibility. Thus, the proposed AI -Enhanced School 
Management System was conceptualized to address these specific issues 
and modernize the overall administrative operations of the school.

 I. System Analysis  
a. Requirements Documentation  
The requirements documentation outlines the essential features and 
system constraints necessary to address the problems observed and 
feedback gathered from staff. The proposed AI -Enhanced School 
Management System includes both functional and non -functional  
requirements.  
Functionally, the system must allow staff to enter student information 
once, which is automatically synced  across all modules through the AI Auto -
Fill Enrollment feature, reducing redundant data entry. It should en able 
recording, updating, and monitoring of academic performance and tuition 
payments in a centralized database. The AI Report Generator will produce 
instant academic and financial reports, eliminating the long hours currently 
spent on manual report prepar ation. An AI Error Detection feature will 
identify inconsistencies or missing information in student records, ensuring 
accuracy and integrity. Staff must be able to quickly search and retrieve 
student records, grade histories, and tuition information to su pport faster 
decision -making. Role -based access will ensure that only authorized 
personnel can edit records while others have view -only permissions.  
Non-functionally, the system must be user -friendly, reliable during 
peak periods such as enrollment or tuition collection, and secure, with login    
 
66 
 authentication and role -based access to safeguard sensitive information. 
The system should be scalable to accommodate a growing number of 
students and administrative tasks, and it must perform all updates, AI auto -
fills, error detection, and report generat ion in real -time to streamline 
operations.  
b. Current System Utilized  
Currently, Calvary Christian Academy relies on a combination of 
manual and semi -digital processes. Student information is stored physically 
in filing cabinets and digitally through spreadsheets and Google Drive, while 
tuition payments are tracked using Exc el and physical receipts. Teachers 
can view student grades through Google Drive, but only administrative staff 
can update records. The IT staff serves  as the system administrator . 
Although this approach ensures that student informat ion is 
eventually recorded, it suffers from redundancy, inefficiency, and a lack of 
real-time access. Administrative staff spend 10 to 15 hours per week 
managing records, and preparing academic or financial reports can take 10 
to 12 hours. These delays hin der timely decision -making and 
communication with parents. The absence of a centralized system limits 
accessibility, scalability, and overall operational efficiency, particularly as 
the number of students and administrative tasks increases.

 Therefore, there is a clear need for a centralized AI -Enhanced 
School Management System to automate repetitive tasks, improve record 
accuracy, streamline report generation, and enhance overall administrative 
operations.

Based on interviews with school administrators and staff,

Christian Academy. These include repetitive data entry across 
multiple forms, lengthy report preparation times, fragmented 
storage of student and tuition records, and limited real -time 
access to essential information. These challenges contribute  to 
increased administrative workload, inefficiency, and delays in 
decision -making.

Management System was designed with both functional and non -
functional requirements in mind.

Enhanced School Management System, showing how users, interfaces,

Interface, which serves as the main entry point for Student/Parent, Teacher, 
Registrar, Admission, Cashier, Principal, and IT Staff. Through this 
interface, users send requests and receive responses, while the User Table 
Database manages user credentials, roles, and access permissions to 
ensure secure authentication and role -based access control.  
Each stakeholder is provided with a dedicated User Interface, 
including the Parents/Student User Interface, Teacher User Interface, 
Registrar User Interface, Admission User Interface, Cashier User Interface, 
and Principal User Interface, allowing role -spec ific interactions with the 
system. These interfaces communicate with the central system by sending 
requests and receiving processed data. The IT Staff  is responsible for 
system maintenance and user account management, ensuring smooth 
operation and system reliability. This structure promotes organized 
communication, improved usability, and efficient system navigation.  
At the core is the AI Processing module, which handles data 
validation, automation, and intelligent processing. It interacts with multiple 
databases such as the Student Database, Enrollment_Form Database,

Database, Risk Assessment Database, and Error_Log Database. Through 
continuous request and response exchanges, the system s upports

tracking. This architecture ensures efficient data flow, real -time processing, 
improved accuracy, and scalability, helping reduce manual workload and 
enhance overall school ope rations.
